How Do I Get Into College?

I can still remember being a Senior in high school, stressing about the complex college application process. That summer going into the last year was spent brainstorming ideas for the common application essay. Knowing that you could write about anything almost made it even harder to hone down on one topic. Thus, I decided to play around and see how an LLM (anthropic/claude-3.5-sonnet) could help make this process a little easier, helping to brainstorm potential prompts and create a college list as well. It’s almost like it is a private $100,000 college counseler. I played around and tried my best to give clear directions and prompts to optimize the LLM’s output according to the PEGAI reading on Chapter 3.

USER

Pretend you are a college counselor. I want help compiling a college list and come up with an idea for the common application essay. Generate a bullet-point list of 10 colleges/universities in the United States with their acceptance rate and a 5 bullet-point list of potential college essay topics.

ASSISTANT

As a college counselor, I’ll help you with a college list and essay topics. However, to provide more personalized recommendations, it would be helpful to know your: - Academic interests/intended major - GPA and test scores - Geographic preferences - Financial considerations - Extracurricular activities

Nevertheless, here’s a diverse list of colleges with varying selectivity:

Colleges/Universities: • Stanford University (CA) - 4% acceptance rate • University of Michigan (MI) - 20% acceptance rate • Boston University (MA) - 14% acceptance rate • University of Wisconsin-Madison (WI) - 60% acceptance rate • Northeastern University (MA) - 18% acceptance rate • University of Texas at Austin (TX) - 32% acceptance rate • University of Virginia (VA) - 21% acceptance rate • Purdue University (IN) - 60% acceptance rate • New York University (NY) - 12% acceptance rate • University of Washington (WA) - 56% acceptance rate

Potential Essay Topics: • A significant challenge you’ve overcome and how it shaped your character • An experience that sparked your intellectual curiosity or passion for learning • A meaningful community service project or leadership experience • A cultural tradition or family value that has influenced your worldview • A moment of failure that led to personal growth and self-discovery

Would you like to share more about your background so I can provide more tailored suggestions?

USER

I don’t feel comfortable providing personal information.

ASSISTANT

I completely understand and respect your privacy. The college list I provided includes a good mix of: - Large research universities - Urban and suburban locations - Different geographic regions - Varying acceptance rates (from highly selective to more accessible) - Both private and public institutions

For the essay topics, here’s some additional context on why these topics work well:

  1. “A significant challenge you’ve overcome”
  • Shows resilience
  • Demonstrates personal growth
  • Reveals character and determination
  1. “An experience that sparked intellectual curiosity”
  • Shows genuine interest in learning
  • Reveals your academic passions
  • Demonstrates intellectual vitality
  1. “A meaningful community service/leadership experience”
  • Shows initiative
  • Demonstrates impact on others
  • Reveals values and priorities
  1. “A cultural tradition/family value”
  • Shows self-awareness
  • Reveals personal background
  • Demonstrates understanding of influences
  1. “A moment of failure”
  • Shows humility
  • Demonstrates ability to learn from mistakes
  • Reveals maturity and self-reflection

Remember that the best essays are: - Personal and specific - Show rather than tell - Authentic to your voice - Reveal something not found elsewhere in your application - Well-written but not overly formal

Would you like me to elaborate on any of these points?

  5. The day my stutter became my superpower: finding confidence through speech therapy and public speaking

  6. An extended metaphor comparing my collection of mismatched socks to embracing life’s imperfections

  7. How teaching my elderly neighbor to use Zoom during the pandemic evolved into a meaningful intergenerational friendship

Each of these topics is: - Specific and personal - Shows character development - Has potential for vivid storytelling - Reveals something meaningful about the writer - Can be developed into a compelling narrative
